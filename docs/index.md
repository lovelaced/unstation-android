# Unstation for Android

Watch live streams and go live from your own camera, straight to your friends. There's no account to
make, nothing to subscribe to, and no company in the middle. Your phone connects directly to the
people you're watching or broadcasting to.

<p align="center"><img src="../assets/screenshots/watch-home.png" alt="The Unstation home screen, where you enter a stream name or open an invite link" width="280"></p>

## First, get set up

You need two apps on your phone, both free:

1. **Unstation** (this app). Grab it from the [releases page](https://github.com/lovelaced/unstation-android/releases) and install it. Your phone may ask you to allow installing apps from outside the store; that's expected.
2. **The Polkadot app.** Unstation uses it to sign you in. Install it from your app store if you don't have it.

Then open Unstation and follow the one-time sign-in: it hands off to the Polkadot app, you tap to
approve, and you're in. This proves you're a real person (which keeps out spam) and gives this phone
permission to connect. Your keys stay in the Polkadot app and never move.

## Is it private? Is it safe?

Streams can be public, unlisted (only people with the link can find them), or invite-only (private, so
even the people helping pass the video along can't watch it). Nobody can tamper with what you see: your
phone checks every stream automatically. The plain-language details are on the main project's
[FAQ](https://lovelaced.github.io/unstation-desktop/faq.html) and
[Security](https://lovelaced.github.io/unstation-desktop/security.html) pages.
