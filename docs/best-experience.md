# Get the best experience

A handful of simple things make streaming and watching much smoother.

## For watching

- **A steady connection helps most.** Wifi is ideal, but cell data works fine. On a weak signal it may
  take a few extra seconds to start and the picture may soften now and then.
- **Turn your phone sideways** for fullscreen. The video fills the screen and the controls tuck away.
- **Sharing is good for everyone.** The more people watching a stream, the smoother it runs for all of
  them, because everyone helps pass the video along. So invite friends freely; it doesn't slow you
  down, it speeds things up.
- **If it won't connect,** give it a moment. On tricky networks (some office wifi, some cell setups) it
  quietly routes through a volunteer helper to reach you, which can take a few seconds longer.

## For going live

- **Use wifi if you can.** Broadcasting sends a lot of video out of your phone, and wifi handles that
  upload better than cell data. On cell data, choose "data saver" quality in Settings.
- **Watch your data.** Broadcasting uses a lot of data, more than watching. If you're on a limited
  plan, keep an eye on it or stick to wifi.
- **Plug in for long streams.** Camera plus encoding plus a live connection is work for your phone, so
  it'll warm up and use battery. A charger keeps you going.
- **Good light, steady hands.** Bright, even light makes the biggest difference to how your stream
  looks. Prop your phone up or use a small stand if you'll be live for a while.
- **Pick the right quality.** High looks great on a strong wifi upload. On cell data or a weaker
  connection, data saver keeps things smooth for your viewers, which matters more than resolution.
- **Close heavy apps** before you go live if your phone feels sluggish, so the camera and encoder have
  room to work.

## A quick word on privacy

If you're streaming something you'd rather keep to a small group, use **unlisted** or **invite-only**
when you name your stream (see [Go live](going-live.md)). And remember that going live is public by
default, so pick the setting that fits before you start.
