# Watch a stream

## Finding a stream

There are two ways in:

- **Open an invite link.** If a friend sent you a link, just tap it. Unstation opens straight into
  their stream. Links look like `unstation://watch/their-stream`.
- **Type the name.** On the home screen, type the stream's name and tap Watch.

<p align="center"><img src="../assets/screenshots/watch-home.png" alt="The home screen with a box to type a stream name and a hint about invite links" width="280"></p>

If a friend's stream is **invite-only**, the name alone won't open it. You need the full invite link
they shared, because it carries the key that unlocks the video.

## While it connects

Unstation tells you what's actually happening. When you start watching, you'll see it work through
finding the broadcaster and connecting to other people watching. This can take a few seconds,
especially on cell data.

<p align="center"><img src="../assets/screenshots/joining-the-mesh.png" alt="The connecting screen showing live progress as it finds the broadcaster and connects to viewers" width="280"></p>

Instead of a spinner that tells you nothing, you might see one of these:

- **Catching up.** It has the stream and is buffering a moment of video. It'll start shortly.
- **Can't reach anyone yet.** Nobody carrying this stream is reachable right now. It keeps trying.
  Double-check the name and that they're actually live.
- **The broadcast ended.** They stopped streaming. You can rejoin if they come back.

## Watching

Once the video is playing, a few controls are one tap away.

<p align="center"><img src="../assets/screenshots/watch-player.png" alt="Watching a live stream, with a tap-for-sound pill and connection details" width="280"></p>

- **Tap for sound.** Streams start muted so they don't blast you. Tap the pill (or the screen) to turn
  sound on.
- **Skip to live.** If you fall behind the live moment, a "skip to live" button jumps you back to the
  front.
- **Rotate for fullscreen.** Turn your phone sideways and the video fills the whole screen.

<p align="center"><img src="../assets/screenshots/landscape-playback.png" alt="Fullscreen landscape playback, the video filling the screen with a small floating overlay" width="640"></p>

## A note on quality

The picture adjusts itself to your connection, so it may look softer for a moment when your signal
dips, then sharpen up. That's normal. For the smoothest watching, see
[Get the best experience](best-experience.md).
