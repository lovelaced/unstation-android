# Help

Common problems and what to do. If none of these fit, the main project's
[FAQ](https://lovelaced.github.io/unstation-desktop/faq.html) has more.

## It won't connect, or says "can't reach anyone"

Check three things: that you've got the stream name right, that the person is actually live right now,
and that your phone has a working connection. Unstation keeps trying on its own, and on tricky networks
it can take a little longer while it finds a way through. If it still won't connect after a minute or
so, the broadcaster may have stopped.

## It says it's connected, but there's no video

The broadcaster has probably stopped streaming. Give it a moment; if the video doesn't come back,
they've likely ended the broadcast, and you'll see the "broadcast ended" screen.

## There's no sound

Streams start muted on purpose. Tap the **tap for sound** pill, or just tap the video, to turn sound
on.

## It says the stream is invite-only

Invite-only streams are private, and the name alone can't open them. Ask the broadcaster for their full
invite link and open that on your phone. The link carries the key that unlocks the video.

## Sign-in didn't finish

Make sure the **Polkadot app** is installed on the same phone. When Unstation hands off to it, approve
the request (you may need to sign, which can take a moment). If it still doesn't complete, try again
from the sign-in screen.

## My phone gets warm or the battery drains while I'm live

That's expected for broadcasting: running the camera and sending video out is real work. Plug in for
longer streams, and try "data saver" quality in Settings. Just watching is much lighter, though the
screen staying on while a video plays is normal.

## The video looks blurry or keeps changing quality

The picture adapts to your connection, so it softens when your signal dips and sharpens when it
recovers. If you're the one broadcasting, your camera quality is in Settings, and it applies the next
time you go live. See [Get the best experience](best-experience.md) for choosing well.

## My broadcast stopped when I switched apps

It shouldn't. Broadcasts keep running in the background with a notification showing. If yours stopped,
your phone may be aggressively closing background apps; check your phone's battery settings and allow
Unstation to run in the background.

## Can I watch on my computer too?

Yes. There's a [desktop app](https://github.com/lovelaced/unstation-desktop) for Mac, Windows, and
Linux that watches and broadcasts the same streams.
