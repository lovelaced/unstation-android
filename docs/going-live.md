# Go live from your phone

## Start broadcasting

1. Tap **Go Live**.
2. **Name your stream.** This is what viewers type to find you, so keep it simple and memorable. The
   app shows you the name people will use as you type.
3. Point your camera and start. You'll see your own preview, and the console flips to live once your
   video is really going out.

<p align="center"><img src="../assets/screenshots/go-live-console.png" alt="The broadcasting console: camera preview, setup checks, viewer count, and live picture quality" width="280"></p>

The console shows you how it's doing: whether you're live, how many people are watching, and how your
video is holding up.

## Share it

Send people to your stream however's easiest:

- **Share the link.** Use the share button to send it through any messaging app.
- **Show a code.** Tap to show a QR code someone can scan with their phone.
- **Just tell them the name.** Anyone can type it in to watch (unless you made it private, below).

## Keep it running

Your broadcast keeps going even if you switch to another app or your screen locks, so you can check a
message without dropping the stream. You'll see a notification while you're live. Come back to the app
and tap to end it when you're done.

## Privacy choices

When you name your stream you can choose how private it is:

- **Public** (the default). Anyone who knows the name can watch.
- **Unlisted.** Adds a secret code to the name so it can't be found by guessing. Only people you send
  the link to can watch.
- **Invite-only.** The strongest option. The video is scrambled so that only people with your invite
  link can see it, and nobody helping carry the stream can watch it.

There's also a **hide my connection** option, which routes viewers through volunteer helpers so they
never see your phone's address directly. It takes a moment longer to get going.

## Camera quality

In **Settings** you can set your camera quality: standard, data saver, or high. Higher looks better
but uses more data and battery. The choice applies the next time you go live. More on choosing well in
[Get the best experience](best-experience.md).
